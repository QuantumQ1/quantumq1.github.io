# Opportunities

We are always looking for motivated researchers to join our group.  
Our research focuses on theoretical high-energy physics, including scattering amplitudes, string theory, quantum gravity, and related areas.  
We welcome applicants with strong interest and background in theoretical physics, mathematics, or related disciplines.

---

## Postdoctoral Positions

We invite applications for **postdoctoral researcher positions** in our group.

**Research areas include:**
- Scattering amplitudes in gauge theory and gravity  
- String theory and effective field theories  
- Worldsheet methods and geometry of amplitudes  
- Holography and conformal field theory  

**Requirements:**
- PhD in theoretical physics or a closely related field  
- Experience in high-energy theory, amplitudes, or mathematical physics  
- Strong publication record and independent research ability

Prospective applicants are encouraged to **contact us directly** with:
- CV  
- List of publications  
- Brief description of research interests  

---

## Research Assistant Positions

We also have openings for **full-time research assistants (RA)**.  
These positions are suitable for students who plan to pursue a PhD in theoretical physics and would like to strengthen their research background.

**Responsibilities:**
- Assist with ongoing research projects  
- Perform symbolic or numerical computations  
- Help maintain group resources or databases (optional)  

**Requirements:**
- Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in physics, mathematics, or related areas  
- Strong motivation for research in high-energy theory  
- Experience with Python/Mathematica/Maple is a plus

Interested candidates may apply by sending:
- CV  
- Transcript (optional)  
- A short statement of interest  

---

## Visiting Students

We welcome **visiting students** (undergraduate, Master, or PhD level) who wish to spend **a short-term research period** in our group.

Visitors can participate in:
- Research projects in amplitudes and string theory  
- Group meetings and seminars  
- Collaborative exploration of new ideas  

Students with strong background in QFT, general relativity, or mathematical physics are especially encouraged.

If you are interested, please contact us with:
- CV  
- Expected visiting period  
- Brief research background
